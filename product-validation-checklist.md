# Product Validation Checklist & Launch-Odds Playbook

The single biggest lever in dropshipping is **product choice** (~80% of the outcome). This is the gate every idea must pass *before* you spend a euro on ads or a store. Score it honestly — the whole point is to kill bad ideas cheaply.

> Use this as a hard filter, not a wish list. If a product fails a **GATE**, stop — don't rationalize. Sunk-cost stubbornness on a dead product is a top way people lose money.

---

## Part 1 — The Scoring System (score before you commit)

Score each criterion. **Max 100.** Be brutal — inflating scores only wastes your own money later.

| # | Criterion | What "good" looks like | Max pts |
|---|---|---|---:|
| 1 | **Margin (3×+ markup)** | Sells at ≥3× landed cost; gross profit/order ≥ €18–20 | 20 |
| 2 | **Wow / problem-solving** | Stops the scroll OR solves a real, felt pain | 15 |
| 3 | **Demand evidence** | Already selling elsewhere (ads running for weeks, high AliExpress orders) | 15 |
| 4 | **Video-demonstrable** | You can show it work / the wow in <15s of video | 10 |
| 5 | **Target audience clarity** | One specific group you can name and target with ads | 10 |
| 6 | **Not in local shops** | Can't be bought at the supermarket / easily offline | 8 |
| 7 | **Shipping & fragility** | Light (<1.5kg), compact, hard to break | 7 |
| 8 | **Competition headroom** | Demand exists but not 50 identical stores already scaling | 7 |
| 9 | **Low regulation/risk** | Not ingestible, electrical-cert, medical, baby, safety-critical | 5 |
| 10 | **Repeat / AOV potential** | Bundles, upsells, or repurchase possible (raises LTV) | 3 |

**Read your score:**
- **80–100** → Strong candidate. Move to validation (Part 2).
- **60–79** → Test only if you can fix the weak criteria (e.g. raise price for margin).
- **< 60** → Pass. Find a better product. There are thousands.

> Margin (line 1) is special: **if you can't hit 3× markup with a healthy gross profit, the product fails regardless of total score.** Ads eat thin margins alive.

## Part 2 — Auto-Reject Red Flags (any one = stop)

- ❌ Markup below ~2.5× / gross profit per order under ~€12.
- ❌ Heavy, bulky, or fragile (shipping cost + breakage + returns kill you).
- ❌ Saturated — dozens of stores already scaling the *identical* product/creative.
- ❌ Trademarked / branded knockoff (legal + instant ad-account bans).
- ❌ Regulated category: supplements/ingestibles, electronics needing CE/FCC, medical claims, baby/child safety, anything that can hurt someone.
- ❌ Supplier under 4.5★ / under 95% positive / low order count.
- ❌ Can't be shown working in a short video (hard to advertise in 2026).
- ❌ Pure seasonal with the season already ending.
- ❌ You can't name who buys it or where to reach them.

## Part 3 — The Validation Workflow (free, ~1–2 days)

Do these **in order**. Stop at the first hard fail.

1. **Margin gate (math first).** Get the landed cost (product + shipping) from AliExpress/CJ. Run it through the unit-economics calculator (`dropshipping-guide.md` §2 or the site calculator). Need 3×+ markup and a positive net/order at a realistic CPA. **Fail here → stop immediately.**
2. **Demand proof.** Search **Meta Ad Library** + **TikTok Creative Center** for the product. *Multiple ads running for weeks* = someone is profitably scaling it = real demand. No ads anywhere = either untested or doesn't sell. Check **AliExpress order counts** and **Amazon Best Sellers**.
3. **Trend direction.** **Google Trends** — is interest rising, flat, or dying? Avoid dying. Note seasonality.
4. **Competition read.** Count how many stores/ads are already scaling it. Some competition = good (validated). A wall of identical scaled stores = you're late; differentiate or pass.
5. **Audience + angle.** Write down: who buys this, the pain/desire, and 1–2 ad angles. If you can't, you can't market it.
6. **Logistics check.** Weight/size/fragility. Find a faster-shipping option (CJ/EU warehouse/Spocket/BigBuy for Spain) — slow shipping = chargebacks.
7. **Legal/risk check (Spain/EU).** Not regulated? No trademark issue? CE marking if electrical?
8. **Test-buy.** Order it yourself. Verify real quality + real delivery time *before* advertising. This single step prevents most refund disasters.

Pass all 8 → build the offer and test ads. Fail any GATE → next product.

## Part 4 — Supplier Vetting Checklist

- [ ] Rating ≥ 4.5★ and ≥ 95% positive feedback.
- [ ] High order volume (proven, not a fresh listing).
- [ ] Read the **bad** reviews specifically — recurring quality/shipping complaints?
- [ ] Realistic shipping time to Spain; tracked shipping line available.
- [ ] You **test-ordered** it and received it.
- [ ] A **backup supplier** identified for the same product.
- [ ] For scale: a **CJ/EU warehouse or local (BigBuy/Spocket)** option with proper IVA invoices.
- [ ] Stable stock (won't vanish mid-campaign).

## Part 5 — Maximize Your Odds (the edges that separate winners)

Picking a decent product is table stakes. These are what actually move you from "lost my test budget" to "profitable."

### Offer > product
The winner usually has the better **offer**, not a different product. Levers:
- **Bundles / volume deals** ("Buy 2 get 1", kits) → higher AOV, better ad math.
- **Free shipping baked into price** (people convert better; just price for it).
- **Honest scarcity / guarantee** (30-day money-back lowers purchase risk).
- **Post-purchase upsells** (ReConvert/Vitals) → free margin on every order.

### Faster shipping = unfair advantage
Most beginners use slow AliExpress lines. Move winners to **CJ / EU warehouse / BigBuy / Spocket** → days not weeks → fewer chargebacks, better reviews, repeat buyers. This alone beats most competitors.

### Creative is the real product
- Make **5–10 video variations** per product; expect 1 to work.
- Nail the **first 3 seconds** (hook). Captions on (muted viewing). 9:16 vertical.
- Mix **real UGC** (buy/film) — it beats AI avatars on winners.
- Model competitor hooks from the Ad Library; don't copy, improve.

### Capital + testing discipline
- Have a **test budget you can afford to lose** (€1,000–2,000). Undercapitalized = panic-quit.
- **Kill losers fast** (below break-even ROAS after enough spend ~1–2× product price). **Scale winners slowly** (~20% steps).
- Track everything daily (use the P&L sheet): spend, sales, ROAS, CPA per ad set.

### Retention = where profit actually lives
- First sale often breaks even; **profit comes from repeat + upsells + email/SMS**.
- Set up **Klaviyo flows**: abandoned checkout, welcome, post-purchase. Cheapest revenue you'll get.

### Trust = conversion
- Reviews (Loox), clear shipping-time statement, real contact info, legal pages, fast mobile site.
- Set shipping expectations **upfront** → fewer "where's my order" tickets and disputes.

### Mindset / metrics
- Treat it as a **marketing business**, not passive income. Show up daily.
- Decide on **data, not feelings.** A product is dead when the numbers say so — move on without ego.
- Reinvest early profit into **more testing**, not lifestyle.
- Protect cash flow: keep IVA/IRPF reserves separate; account for payment-processor holds.

## Part 6 — One-Page Go/No-Go

```
GO only if ALL true:
  [ ] Score ≥ 80 (or ≥60 with a clear fix)
  [ ] 3×+ markup AND positive net/order at realistic CPA
  [ ] Demand proven (ads running for weeks / high orders)
  [ ] No auto-reject red flag
  [ ] You can name the audience + a video angle
  [ ] A faster-shipping path exists
  [ ] Not regulated / no trademark issue
  [ ] You test-ordered and received it

NO-GO → next product. No exceptions, no rationalizing.
```
