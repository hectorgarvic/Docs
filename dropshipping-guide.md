# Dropshipping: A No-Bullshit Starter Guide

*Written from the perspective of someone who has run e-commerce stores and managed P&Ls. Read the reality check first. If it scares you off, it saved you money.*

---

## 0. Reality Check (Read This or Lose Money)

Before anything: **most dropshipping stores lose money and shut down.** This is not pessimism, it's the base rate. The "passive income, laptop on a beach" content you've seen on YouTube/TikTok is mostly people selling courses, not people selling products. Their real product is *you*.

What's actually true in 2026:

- **It is a real business, not passive income.** You are a marketer and a customer-service operation, not a "store owner" in any relaxed sense.
- **Margins are thin and ad costs are high.** Meta/TikTok ad costs keep rising. You compete against thousands doing the exact same thing with the exact same AliExpress product.
- **Slow shipping kills trust.** Classic AliExpress dropshipping = 2–4 week delivery. Customers hate it, chargebacks follow.
- **Your edge is marketing and brand, not the product.** Anyone can list the same gadget. The winner has better ads, better positioning, faster shipping, better service.
- **You need real cash to test.** Budget €1,000–€2,000 minimum to *test* properly. Less than that and you can't gather enough data to know what works. This money can disappear with zero return. Treat it as tuition.

**If you can't afford to lose your test budget, don't start yet.** Save first.

Honest expectation: out of every 10 products you test, maybe 1 shows promise. Out of stores started, a small minority become profitable. Plan for failure as the default and be pleasantly surprised.

## 1. What Dropshipping Actually Is

You sell a product online. When a customer buys, you forward the order to a supplier who ships it directly to the customer. You never hold inventory.

```
Customer → pays you €30 → You → pay supplier €10 → Supplier ships to customer
Your gross = €20, minus ads (~€10-15), minus fees, minus refunds = thin net
```

**The catch:** you never touch the product, so quality and shipping are out of your hands but the complaints land on you. Your job is to choose suppliers carefully and front the marketing.

### Variants worth knowing
- **Classic (AliExpress):** cheapest entry, slowest shipping, lowest trust. Hardest mode now.
- **Agent / private supplier:** once a product sells, you get a sourcing agent (e.g. via CJ Dropshipping or a dedicated agent) for faster shipping and better pricing. This is where serious sellers go.
- **Print on Demand (POD):** custom designs on mugs/shirts/posters (Printful, Printify). Lower competition on the *design*, but you still need marketing.
- **Local/domestic suppliers:** faster shipping, higher cost, much better customer experience. Best for building a real brand.

## 2. The Financials (The Part Most Guides Skip)

You are running a P&L. Internalize this math before spending a euro on ads.

### Unit economics per order
| Item | Example |
|---|---|
| Selling price | €34.99 |
| Product cost (COGS) | €9.00 |
| Shipping (supplier) | €3.00 |
| Payment processing (~3%) | €1.05 |
| **Gross profit / order** | **€21.94** |
| Cost to acquire customer (CPA via ads) | €15.00 |
| Refunds/chargebacks reserve (~5%) | €1.75 |
| **Net contribution / order** | **≈ €5.19** |

### The number that decides everything: Break-even ROAS
**ROAS** = Return On Ad Spend = revenue ÷ ad spend.

```
Break-even ROAS = Selling price ÷ Gross profit per order (before ads)
Example: 34.99 ÷ (34.99 - 9 - 3 - 1.05) = 34.99 / 21.94 ≈ 1.59
```

So you need a ROAS above **~1.6** just to not lose money on ads. To actually profit and cover your time/overhead, target ROAS **2.0+**. If your ads return less than break-even, you are paying to lose money — kill the ad.

### Key metrics to track from day one
- **CPA** (Cost Per Acquisition): ad cost to get one sale.
- **AOV** (Average Order Value): raise it with bundles/upsells.
- **Conversion rate**: visitors who buy (1–3% is normal).
- **ROAS**: per ad set, daily.
- **Contribution margin**: what's left after ALL variable costs. This must be positive.
- **Cash flow**: you pay ad costs *now*, supplier *now*, but payment processors may hold your money for days/weeks. Stripe/PayPal can freeze new accounts. **Undercapitalized stores die from cash-flow timing, not lack of profit.**

### Pricing rule of thumb
Sell at **3x to 4x** your landed product cost (product + shipping). Below 3x, ads eat your margin. This is why €5–€15 product cost items sold at €25–€50 are the sweet spot.

## 3. Choosing a Product (Where Stores Are Won or Lost)

Product selection is ~80% of the outcome. Don't sell what you like; sell what a defined audience will impulse-buy.

### Criteria for a good dropshipping product
1. **Solves a real problem or has a strong "wow" factor.** (Pain reliever, or scroll-stopping.)
2. **Not easily found in local shops.** If they can buy it at the supermarket, they will.
3. **Profit margin allows 3x+ markup.** Product cost €5–€20 ideal.
4. **Lightweight & not fragile.** Cheap shipping, fewer breakages/returns.
5. **Has a clear target audience** you can target with ads.
6. **Demonstrable in video.** Modern ads = short video. If you can't show it working in 15 seconds, it's hard.
7. **Not heavily regulated.** Avoid: ingestibles, supplements, electronics needing certification (CE/FCC), anything safety-critical, anything for babies/medical. Legal risk + ad account bans.
8. **Not seasonal-only** (unless you accept the short window).

### Where to find products
- **TikTok / Reels:** search what's going viral, "TikTok made me buy it".
- **AliExpress / CJ Dropshipping:** check order volume and reviews.
- **Amazon Movers & Shakers / Best Sellers:** demand validation.
- **Competitor ad libraries:** **Meta Ad Library** (free) — search active ads; many running ads = there's a market (and competition). **TikTok Creative Center**.
- **Spy tools** (paid, optional): Minea, PiPiAds, Sell The Trend. Useful but not required to start.

### Red flags / avoid
- Saturated products everyone is already running (you arrive late, ads are expensive).
- Trademarked/branded knockoffs (legal trouble, account bans).
- Anything over ~1.5kg or fragile or expensive to return.
- Products with bad supplier reviews (<4.5 stars, <95% positive).

**Validation before committing:** is there proven demand (people already buying)? Can you reach the audience profitably with ads? Can you get it shipped in a reasonable time? If any answer is no, move on.

## 4. Setting Up the Store

### Platform
- **Shopify** — default choice. ~€29–€39/mo + transaction fees. Easiest, most apps, best support. Start here.
- **WooCommerce** (WordPress) — cheaper long-term, more technical, you manage hosting. Only if you're comfortable with tech.
- Avoid building custom — waste of time at the start.

### Core apps/tools
- **DSers** or **CJ Dropshipping** — connect supplier, auto-fulfill orders.
- **A review app** (Loox, Judge.me) — social proof is critical for trust.
- **Currency/region** settings correct for your market.
- **Klaviyo** (email) — abandoned-cart + post-purchase flows recover real revenue. Set up later but soon.

### A store that converts has
- **One clear hero product** (start with a single product or tight niche, not a 500-item junk store).
- **Strong product page:** benefit-led copy, multiple images, demo video, reviews, clear price, scarcity if honest.
- **Trust signals:** reviews, clear returns policy, contact info, secure-checkout badges, FAQ.
- **Fast mobile load** (most traffic is mobile; slow = lost sales).
- **Simple checkout** with the payment methods your market uses (card, PayPal, and local methods — e.g. Bizum in Spain, Klarna in parts of EU).

### Don't skip (boring but mandatory)
- **Legal pages:** Terms, Privacy Policy, Refund/Returns, Shipping policy. Ad platforms require them; customers check them.
- **Business registration & taxes:** register as self-employed/business per your country (in Spain: *autónomo* + IVA). **VAT/sales tax** applies. EU has rules on distance selling and the **IOSS** scheme for imports. Talk to an accountant *before* scaling — this is not optional and getting it wrong is expensive. (Fact: rules vary by country; this guide is not legal/tax advice.)
- **Customs/duties:** imports from China may incur duties; EU removed the low-value VAT exemption — budget for it.

## 5. Advertising (Where the Money Goes)

This is the engine. Most of your budget and skill goes here.

### Main channels
- **TikTok Ads** — best for cheap impulse/wow products, younger audience, video-native. Often cheapest CPMs for the right product.
- **Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Ads** — broadest reach, powerful targeting/optimization, works across ages. The workhorse.
- **Organic TikTok/Reels** — post your own short videos; if one goes viral, you get near-free traffic. Highest-leverage, slowest/unreliable. Worth doing alongside paid.
- **Google Shopping** — for products people actively search for (less impulse, more intent). Different game.

### The creative is everything
In 2026, **the ad creative (the video) matters more than targeting.** Platforms auto-target well; they can't fix a boring video.

A good direct-response video ad (15–30s):
1. **Hook (first 3 seconds):** stop the scroll. Show the problem or the wow moment immediately.
2. **Problem/agitation:** the pain the product solves.
3. **Solution/demo:** show it working.
4. **Social proof:** reviews, "10,000 sold".
5. **Call to action:** "Tap to get yours, 50% off today."

Make **multiple creatives** (5–10 variations). Most will flop; you're hunting for the 1 that works. This is normal.

### How to actually test (don't set €100/day on one ad)
1. Start small: e.g. €10–€20/day per ad set, or a small CBO campaign.
2. Test several creatives/audiences cheaply.
3. Give it data: don't kill an ad after 6 hours. Let it spend ~1–2x your product price before judging (need enough impressions/clicks).
4. **Kill losers fast, scale winners slowly.** If an ad set is below break-even ROAS after enough spend, cut it. Scale winners by ~20% increments — scaling too fast tanks performance.
5. Track everything in a simple sheet: spend, sales, ROAS, CPA per ad set, daily.

### Watch out
- Ad accounts get **banned/restricted** easily (especially new ones, especially for policy violations — health claims, before/after, trademark). Have a backup plan and never lie in ads.
- **iOS tracking limits** make attribution fuzzy — use the platform's tools + post-purchase surveys ("how did you hear about us?").

## 6. Operations: Keeping It Running

Selling is only half. The other half is not drowning in problems.

- **Customer service:** answer fast. "Where is my order?" is 80% of tickets — set expectations on shipping time *upfront* on the product page to reduce this. Slow shipping + silence = chargebacks.
- **Order fulfillment:** automate via DSers/CJ, but check daily that orders actually ship and tracking updates.
- **Refunds & disputes:** have a clear, fair policy. A refund is cheaper than a chargeback (chargebacks hurt your payment account). Keep chargeback rate low or processors freeze you.
- **Supplier reliability:** order the product yourself first (test buy) to verify quality and shipping time. Re-check periodically. Have a backup supplier.
- **Cash flow management:** track money in vs. out *weekly*. Don't spend profit you haven't actually received (processor holds). This kills more stores than bad products.
- **Reinvest, don't splurge:** early profit goes back into testing more products and creatives.

## 7. A Realistic Step-by-Step Plan

**Phase 0 — Before spending (1–2 weeks)**
- Save your test budget (€1,000–€2,000 you can afford to lose).
- Learn the basics (free: YouTube creators who *show their stores/ad accounts*, not just talk). Skip paid courses at first.
- Register your business/tax status or at least understand the requirements.

**Phase 1 — Setup (1 week)**
- Pick ONE product after real validation (demand + margin + shippable + ad-able).
- Build a clean one-product (or tight niche) Shopify store with all legal pages.
- Connect supplier (DSers/CJ). Do a test purchase yourself.
- Set up payment processing and confirm it works.

**Phase 2 — First test (2–4 weeks)**
- Make/order 5–10 ad creatives.
- Run small paid tests (TikTok and/or Meta). Track ROAS/CPA daily.
- Kill losers, note what hooks work.
- Expect to lose money here. You're buying data.

**Phase 3 — Decide**
- **Profitable / promising?** Scale winners slowly, get a sourcing agent for faster shipping, add upsells/email flows, make more creatives.
- **Not working after honest testing?** Kill the product. Take the lesson. Test the next one. *Do not* pour more money into a dead product out of stubbornness (sunk-cost fallacy ruins people).

**Phase 4 — Build a real business (if it works)**
- Move from random products to a **brand/niche** with repeat customers.
- Faster shipping (agent/local), better packaging, email/SMS retention.
- Track P&L properly. Pay your taxes. Consider an accountant.

## 8. Common Ways People Lose Money (Avoid These)

1. **No test budget / underfunded** — can't gather data, panic-quit. → Save first.
2. **Falling for guru courses** — the "system" is the product they sell. → Learn free, spend on ads instead.
3. **Bad product, great everything else** — product choice is 80%. → Validate ruthlessly.
4. **Killing ads too fast or scaling too fast** — both lose money. → Patience on testing, slow scaling.
5. **Ignoring unit economics** — selling at 2x cost, ads eat it all. → 3x+ markup, know your break-even ROAS.
6. **Slow shipping + bad service** — chargebacks, banned payment account. → Set expectations, get faster suppliers, answer customers.
7. **Sunk-cost stubbornness** — flogging a dead product. → Cut and move on.
8. **Skipping legal/tax** — fines, frozen accounts. → Register, get IOSS/VAT right, consult an accountant.
9. **Lying in ads** (fake claims, before/after) — account bans, legal risk. → Honest marketing only.
10. **Treating it as passive** — it's an active marketing business. → Show up daily.

## 9. Quick Glossary

- **COGS** — Cost of Goods Sold (product + supplier shipping).
- **AOV** — Average Order Value.
- **CPA / CAC** — Cost Per Acquisition / Customer Acquisition Cost.
- **ROAS** — Return On Ad Spend (revenue ÷ ad spend).
- **CPM** — Cost Per 1,000 impressions.
- **CTR** — Click-Through Rate.
- **CR** — Conversion Rate.
- **Contribution margin** — revenue minus all variable costs per order.
- **POD** — Print On Demand.
- **CBO / ABO** — Campaign / Ad-set Budget Optimization (Meta).
- **Chargeback** — customer disputes a charge with their bank; bad for your payment account.
- **IOSS** — EU Import One-Stop-Shop for VAT on imports.

## 10. Bottom Line

Dropshipping is a **real, hard, marketing-driven business** with a high failure rate and thin margins. The people who win:
- Pick validated products with healthy margins,
- Make great video ads and test cheaply,
- Watch their unit economics like a hawk,
- Ship reasonably fast and treat customers well,
- Cut losers fast and reinvest in winners,
- Handle tax/legal properly.

If you go in expecting easy passive money, you'll be one of the many who lose their budget. If you go in treating it as a serious business with a test-and-learn loop and money you can afford to risk, you have a real shot.

*This guide is educational, not financial, legal, or tax advice. Tax/legal rules vary by country — consult a qualified accountant for your situation.*

## Annex A — Spain: Tax & Legal (Verified June 2026)

> **Not legal/tax advice. Figures verified June 2026 but rules and amounts change — confirm with a *gestor*/*asesor fiscal* and the official sources (Agencia Tributaria, Seguridad Social) before acting.**

### The big misconception: "under X € you don't need to be autónomo"

This is **half true and dangerous**, because it confuses **two separate registrations**:

| Registration | What it's for | Income minimum? |
|---|---|---|
| **Hacienda** — *alta censal* (modelo **036/037**) | Lets you legally invoice, and charge/declare **IVA** and **IRPF** | **None. Always required if you carry out economic activity.** No exemption by low income. |
| **Seguridad Social** — **RETA** (the autónomo *cuota*) | Social security contribution | This is where the SMI debate lives. |

So even with tiny income, **if you sell, you must register with Hacienda.** There is no "free pass" tier for that.

### The SMI / RETA nuance (this is the real source of the myth)

- There is **no clean legal income threshold** in the law that exempts you from RETA. The "under SMI you don't need RETA" idea comes from **Tribunal Supremo case law (2007)**: if your income is below the **SMI** and the activity isn't *habitual*, you may not be obliged to register in RETA.
- **The legal test is "habitualidad", not the amount.** If the activity is "habitual, personal y directa" and lucrative, you must register in RETA — *regardless of how little you earn*.
- **A dropshipping store is habitual by nature** — daily ad management, an always-open store, continuous orders. That points strongly toward **needing to be autónomo**, even below SMI.
- Relying on the under-SMI argument is **risky**: it's interpretation, not a guaranteed right. If *Inspección* decides your activity was habitual, you can owe **back cuotas + surcharges (recargo) + penalties**.

**2026 reference figures (verified June 2026):**
- **SMI 2026:** ~**€1.221/month** gross (14 payments/year). *(Used only as the reference some case law cites — not a magic "tax-free" line.)*
- **Tarifa plana:** **€80/month for the first 12 months** for new autónomos (can extend a further 12 months if net income stays under SMI). Big reason starting *as autónomo* is cheaper than people assume.
- **Cuota by real income (system since 2023):** you contribute by **net income tranches**. Lowest tranches roughly **€200–€260/month** after the flat-rate period (2026 cuotas largely held at 2025 levels + small MEI rise of +0,1%). Higher income = higher cuota.

### What you actually have to deal with (Spain)

1. **Alta en Hacienda (036/037):** pick your *epígrafe IAE* (e-commerce / *comercio al por menor*). Do this **before** your first sale.
2. **Alta en RETA (autónomo):** for an ongoing dropshipping business, plan for this. Use **tarifa plana €80/mes** at the start.
3. **IVA:**
   - Charge Spanish **IVA (21%)** on sales to Spanish/EU consumers (with EU distance-selling rules — see OSS below).
   - File **modelo 303** (quarterly) and **modelo 390** (annual summary).
4. **IRPF (income tax):**
   - Quarterly **modelo 130** (pago fraccionado, ~20% of net profit) under *estimación directa*.
   - Annual **declaración de la renta**.
5. **Imports from China (this bites dropshippers):**
   - The EU removed the **<€22 VAT exemption**. **IVA is due on essentially all imports**, plus **customs duties** above €150.
   - **IOSS** (Import One-Stop-Shop) lets you collect/remit import VAT at checkout for consignments **≤ €150** — smoother customs, fewer surprise fees for customers. Worth setting up if importing low-value goods.
6. **OSS (One-Stop-Shop)** for **B2C sales to other EU countries**: once you pass the EU-wide **€10.000/year** distance-sales threshold, you charge the **customer's country VAT** and file via OSS instead of registering in each country.
7. **Modelo 349** if you have intra-EU transactions with VAT-registered parties.
8. **RGPD/LOPD (GDPR):** privacy policy, cookie banner, lawful basis for marketing emails. Mandatory for any store with EU customers.
9. **Consumer law:** **14-day right of withdrawal** (*derecho de desistimiento*) for distance sales is legally required in the EU — your returns policy must reflect it.

### Practical Spain takeaway

- **You cannot legally run an ongoing dropshipping store in Spain with zero registration.** Hacienda: always. RETA: almost certainly, because it's habitual.
- The "under SMI" loophole is for *occasional, non-habitual* activity — not a real business you advertise daily.
- **Get a *gestor*** (~€40–€80/month). For the volume of modelos (036, 303, 130, 390, possibly IOSS/OSS/349) it pays for itself and keeps you out of trouble. This is the single best early expense after your ad budget.

### Worked example — a €5.000/month store (Spain, exact numbers)

> **The business numbers below are illustrative assumptions. The tax rates/rules (IVA 21%, IRPF ~20% fractioned, cuota figures) are real and verified June 2026.** Your actual IRPF depends on your total annual income and bracket — modelo 130 is a *fractioned payment on account*, not your final tax.

**Setup of the example:**
- Sells to Spanish consumers. Customer price **€34.99 (IVA included)**.
- **173 orders/month** → gross collected = 173 × €34.99 = **€6.053** (round to €6.050).
- Spain B2C → price includes **21% IVA**.

**Step 1 — Split the IVA out (the price includes it; it was never your money):**
```
Revenue ex-IVA      = 6.050 / 1,21        = €5.000,00
IVA collected (out) = 6.050 − 5.000       = €1.050,00   → owed to Hacienda
```

**Step 2 — Monthly P&L on the ex-IVA basis (the correct way):**
| Line | Amount | Note |
|---|---:|---|
| Revenue (ex-IVA) | €5.000,00 | |
| COGS (landed €11 × 173) | −€1.903,00 | product + supplier shipping |
| Ad spend (Meta/TikTok) | −€1.800,00 | ROAS ≈ 6.050/1.800 ≈ **3,36** (healthy) |
| Payment fees (3% of €6.050) | −€181,50 | charged on gross incl. IVA |
| Shopify + apps | −€60,00 | |
| Gestor | −€70,00 | |
| Cuota autónomo (tarifa plana, yr 1) | −€80,00 | |
| **Net profit before IRPF** | **€905,50** | |

**Step 3 — IRPF set-aside (modelo 130, 20% of net profit):**
```
IRPF to reserve = 905,50 × 0,20 = €181,10
Take-home (year 1)             = 905,50 − 181,10 = €724,40 / month
```

**Step 4 — After tarifa plana ends (cuota rises to ~€230/mes, low income tranche):**
```
Net before IRPF = 905,50 − (230 − 80)  = €755,50
IRPF (20%)      = 151,10
Take-home       = €604,40 / month
```

**The honest takeaway:** a **€5.000/month (ex-IVA) store** at a healthy 3.36 ROAS nets you roughly **€600–€725/month** take-home. The "€5k store" headline is **6x larger** than what you keep. This is why ad efficiency (ROAS) and margin are everything — a small ROAS drop wipes the net out entirely.

**Step 5 — What you must set aside and pay (don't spend it):**
| Tax | When | Roughly (this example) |
|---|---|---|
| **IVA** (modelo 303) | Quarterly | ~€1.050/mo collected → ~**€3.150/quarter** to Hacienda (less any deductible input IVA — see caveat). |
| **IRPF** (modelo 130) | Quarterly | ~€181/mo → ~**€543/quarter** payment on account. |
| **Cuota autónomo** | Monthly | €80 (yr 1) → ~€230+ after. |

**Important IVA caveat for dropshipping (read this):**
This example conservatively assumes **~€0 deductible input IVA**, because:
- **Foreign suppliers** (AliExpress/CJ, China) don't give you a Spanish IVA invoice — in true direct-from-China dropshipping the import VAT/customs handling is messy and you're often not the importer of record. Don't assume you can deduct it.
- **Ads (Meta/TikTok Ireland) and Shopify (Ireland)** are **intra-EU B2B services → reverse charge**: you self-assess and simultaneously deduct the IVA, so it nets to **€0 cash** but you must declare it (and file **modelo 349**).

So in practice you remit close to the **full €1.050/mo** of collected IVA. If you instead source from a **Spanish/EU VAT-registered supplier** with proper invoices, you'd have deductible input IVA and remit less — another reason local suppliers are worth it. **A gestor will get this right; doing it by gut will not.**

A ready-to-use spreadsheet ships alongside this guide: **`dropshipping-pl-template.csv`** (open in Excel / Google Sheets / LibreOffice).

How to use the key formulas (in case the CSV formulas don't import in your tool):
- `Landed cost = Product cost + Supplier shipping`
- `Gross profit/order = Selling price − Landed cost − Payment fee`
- `Break-even ROAS = Selling price ÷ Gross profit/order`
- `Net/order = Gross profit/order − CPA − Refund reserve`
- `Tax set-aside ≈ 21% IVA (collected, not yours) + ~20% IRPF on net profit` — **keep this money separate; it is not profit.**

- **Alibaba** — bulk/private label once you scale a winner.
- **For Spain/EU buyers:** prioritize EU-based suppliers (Spocket, BigBuy — Spanish dropshipping supplier) to cut shipping time and get proper IVA invoices.

### Ads & analytics
- **Meta Ads Manager, TikTok Ads Manager** — the two main platforms.
- **Google Ads / Merchant Center** — for search-intent products.
- **TripleWhale / Lifetimely** — profit + attribution dashboards (later, when volume justifies).
- **Post-purchase survey** ("how did you hear about us") — fixes iOS attribution blind spots cheaply.

### Spain: admin & finance
- **Gestoría online:** Declarando, Quipu, Cuéntica, Getquipu, TaxScouts — handle modelos 036/303/130, invoicing, some include a gestor. ~€10–€80/mo.
- **Banking:** a **separate business account** (Qonto, N26 Business, BBVA/Sabadell autónomo accounts) — keep IVA/IRPF reserves out of your personal money.
- **Official sources (always the final word):** [Agencia Tributaria](https://sede.agenciatributaria.gob.es), [Seguridad Social](https://www.seg-social.es).

### Learning (free > paid courses)
- **YouTube:** prefer creators who **show real ad accounts/stores**, not just talk. Be skeptical of anyone whose main product is a course.
- **Shopify Compass / Shopify blog** — solid free fundamentals.
- **Reddit:** r/dropship, r/ecommerce — real failures and lessons, less hype.
- **Skip:** expensive "mentorship" programs and Discord "communities" charging monthly. The information is free; they sell hope.

## Annex D — Setup / Configuration Guide (per tool)

> **UIs change constantly.** Exact button names and menu locations will differ from what's written here — these are the *flows* and the *settings that actually matter*, not a click-by-click that goes stale in a month. When a step here doesn't match the screen, search "[tool] + [the thing you're doing] 2026". Do these **in the order below** — each depends on the previous.

### Setup order (do not skip ahead)
```
1. Business/tax (Hacienda) → 2. Bank account → 3. Shopify store
→ 4. Supplier app → 5. Payments → 6. Legal pages → 7. Pixels/tracking
→ 8. Ad accounts → 9. AI/creative tools → 10. Email → THEN launch ads
```

---

### 1. Spain admin (BEFORE selling anything)
1. **Alta en Hacienda** — file **modelo 036/037** online (with *certificado digital* or Cl@ve). Pick *epígrafe IAE* for e-commerce/*comercio al por menor*. Declare you'll do intra-EU operations (for ads reverse-charge) → get a **VAT-EU number (ROI)** via modelo 036 if you'll use IOSS/OSS or buy ads from Ireland.
2. **Alta en RETA** (autónomo) — via Seguridad Social, request **tarifa plana €80/mes**. Do this the same day you start activity.
3. **Get a gestor** (Declarando/Quipu/Cuéntica) — connect your bank + Shopify so modelos 303/130 are semi-automatic.
> If steps 1–2 read as overwhelming, that's exactly what the gestor is for — €40–80/mo to handle it. Pay it.

### 2. Business bank account
- Open a **separate account** (Qonto/N26 Business/bank autónomo account).
- **Rule:** open 3 "buckets" (sub-accounts or a spreadsheet): **Operating**, **IVA reserve**, **IRPF reserve**. Every sale, mentally move the 21% IVA + ~20%-of-profit IRPF into reserves immediately. This single habit prevents the #1 killer (spending tax money).

### 3. Shopify store
1. **Sign up** at shopify.com → start free trial. Choose the **Basic** plan to launch.
2. **Settings → Store details:** set country = Spain, currency = EUR, timezone.
3. **Settings → Taxes:** enable **IVA collection for Spain (21%)**. Decide **prices include tax** (normal for EU consumers) — set "All prices include tax".
4. **Theme:** install a free fast theme (Dawn) or a clean paid one. Mobile-first.
5. **Build the product page** (the page that sells): hero image + demo video, benefit-led bullets, price, reviews block, trust badges, FAQ, clear shipping-time statement.
6. **Navigation:** keep it minimal — Home, Product(s), FAQ, Contact, Track Order.
7. **Domain:** buy a clean .com (Settings → Domains). Avoid weird hyphenated names.
8. **Speed:** compress images, limit apps. Test on phone.

### 4. Supplier app (DSers or CJ Dropshipping)
**DSers (AliExpress):**
1. Install **DSers** from Shopify App Store → link AliExpress account.
2. Import product → push to Shopify → set your selling price (3–4× landed cost).
3. Map the **correct variant/shipping method** (choose a tracked, faster line, e.g. AliExpress Standard/CHOICE).
4. Enable **auto-fulfillment** so paid orders forward to supplier automatically.
5. **Always do a test order yourself** — verify product quality + real delivery time before advertising.

**CJ Dropshipping (when scaling a winner):**
1. Create CJ account → connect Shopify.
2. Search product by name/image → request **sourcing + a quote** (often cheaper + faster than AliExpress).
3. Set up **EU/local warehouse** shipping if available (cuts delivery to days).
4. For Spain speed, also check **BigBuy** or **Spocket** (EU suppliers, proper IVA invoices).

### 5. Payments
1. **Shopify Payments** (Stripe-based) — enable in Settings → Payments. Fastest. Add company/autónomo details.
2. Add **PayPal** (Spanish buyers expect it) and a **local method** (Bizum where supported).
3. **Payout schedule + holds:** new accounts may have funds held days/weeks. **Don't budget ad spend assuming instant cash.**
4. Keep **chargeback rate low** — clear shipping times + fast support; processors freeze high-dispute stores.

### 6. Legal pages (Shopify can generate, then edit)
- Settings → Policies: generate **Refund/Returns, Privacy, Terms, Shipping**. Edit to be truthful.
- Add **14-day right of withdrawal** (EU *derecho de desistimiento*) to returns.
- Add **cookie banner + privacy** (GDPR) — app like "GDPR/Cookie Banner".
- Show **contact email + business identity** (legally required, also builds trust).

### 7. Pixels & tracking (do BEFORE running ads — or you fly blind)
**Meta Pixel + Conversions API:**
1. Create a **Meta Business Manager** account → create a **Pixel/Dataset**.
2. Install **Facebook & Instagram** Shopify app → connect Business Manager → it wires the **Pixel + Conversions API (CAPI)** automatically. CAPI matters post-iOS — don't skip it.
3. Verify your **domain** in Business Manager.
4. Confirm events fire: **ViewContent, AddToCart, InitiateCheckout, Purchase** (use Meta's Events Manager test tool).

**TikTok Pixel:**
1. Create **TikTok Ads Manager** account → create Pixel/Events.
2. Install **TikTok** Shopify app → connect → enable **Events API** (server-side).
3. Test events fire.

### 8. Ad accounts
- **Meta:** in Business Manager create an **Ad Account**, add payment method, set country/currency/timezone (can't change timezone later — set correctly). **Warm up** new accounts (start low spend) to reduce bans.
- **TikTok:** Ads Manager → create ad account → add billing.
- **Avoid instant bans:** verified domain, real business info, no health/medical/before-after claims, no trademark misuse.

### 9. AI & creative tools setup
- **ChatGPT / Claude:** no real "config" — build reusable prompts. Example: *"Write 5 TikTok ad hooks (first 3 seconds) for [product] targeting [audience]. Problem-led, scroll-stopping, under 12 words."* Save your good prompts.
- **CapCut:** install → use trending templates → cut to 15–30s → captions on (most watch muted) → export 9:16 vertical.
- **AI UGC (Arcads/HeyGen/Creatify):** sign up → pick avatar → paste your script (generate script with ChatGPT/Claude) → export. **Test these cheaply; expect real human UGC to beat them on winners.**
- **ElevenLabs:** clone/pick a voice → paste script → export voiceover for the CapCut edit.
- **Midjourney/Firefly:** generate lifestyle/background images. Don't fake the actual product — misleading creative = refunds + bans.
- **Canva:** logo, banners, simple statics. Use brand colors consistently.

### 10. Email/SMS (Klaviyo) — set up before scaling
1. Install **Klaviyo** Shopify app → sync.
2. Turn on the 3 money flows:
   - **Abandoned checkout** (recovers lost sales — highest ROI).
   - **Welcome/first-purchase** discount.
   - **Post-purchase** (shipping expectations + review request + cross-sell).
3. Get explicit **consent** (GDPR) — checkbox, not pre-ticked.

### 11. Research tools setup (how to actually use them)
- **Meta Ad Library** (free): go to facebook.com/ads/library → filter country + "All ads" → search your product/niche → **many active ads running for weeks = proven demand** (and competition). Study their hooks.
- **TikTok Creative Center** (free): browse Top Ads/Trends by region → see what's converting.
- **Google Trends:** check the product term is **rising, not dying**, and seasonality.
- **AliExpress:** sort by orders → high orders + 4.5★+ + 95%+ positive = safer supplier.
- **Spy tools (Minea/PiPiAds)** if you pay: filter by "newest/scaling" ads in your geo, sort by engagement, save winners to a swipe file.

### Pre-launch checklist (don't run ads until all ✅)
- [ ] Hacienda alta done; gestor connected
- [ ] Separate bank account + IVA/IRPF buckets
- [ ] Store live, mobile-fast, one strong product page
- [ ] Supplier connected + **test order placed and received**
- [ ] Payments working (card + PayPal)
- [ ] All legal pages + cookie banner live
- [ ] Meta Pixel **+ CAPI** and/or TikTok Pixel firing Purchase events (tested)
- [ ] 5–10 ad creatives ready
- [ ] Tracking sheet (Annex B) open
- [ ] Test budget you can afford to lose, set aside
